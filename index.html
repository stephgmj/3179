Renewable Energy in Victoria

Types of Renewable Energy

Victoria generates renewable energy through a few different sources. Wind farms are currently the largest producer of renewable energy in Victoria.

The major wind farms in Victoria are located in areas that are generally higher altitude and therefore, on average, see more consistently higher wind speeds than other areas. This means that as much energy as possible can be produced by these farms.

We are seeing an increase in the usage of solar panels, in both both small-scale (personal) and commercial use. As the technology has become more and more affordable, more people have been inclined to install solar panels on their houses so they can also reduce the cost of their electricity.

Over the years...

While the majority of Victoria's energy is being produced by coal, we are seeing a steady increase in energy being produced by renewable means, especially wind.

With this increase in renewable energy production, there has also been an overall decrease in the amount of energy being produced by burning coal.

What does our energy generation look like over a year?

Over the year, we can see that the amount of energy being produced in Victoria increases for the major energy types during the winter months.

This is to meet demand due to the lesser daylight hours and the increased need for electricity for appliances like heaters during this time.

We can also see an increase in the amount of energy being generated by rooftop solar panels during the summer months, thanks to longer days and more sunny days overall compared to winter.
